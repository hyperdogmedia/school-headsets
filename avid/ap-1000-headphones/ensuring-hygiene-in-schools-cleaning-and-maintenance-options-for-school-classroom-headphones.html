Noise-Canceling Microphones

Noise-canceling microphones are an essential feature for headsets that are used in noisy environments like classrooms and office spaces. These microphones are designed for filtering out ambient noise, which allows the user to concentrate on their work or listen music without distraction. They also eliminate background noise during calls. They come in a range of sizes that work with most head shapes and are compatible with phones with 3.5mm connections.

One popular technology for noise-canceling headphones is a dual-microphone design that employs two microphones that are directional to block out sounds from the surrounding. The primary microphone is situated close to the source of sound such as the user's voice or their music. The second microphone is used to record ambient sounds like air traffic and other voices. When the two signals are subtracted from one other, the majority of background noise is canceled out leaving only the desired audio signal.

Another headset that incorporates noise cancellation technology is a hybrid system. The headset uses an electronic processor to analyze sounds and determine what frequencies should be eliminated from the audio. The headset then filters out the particular frequencies.
