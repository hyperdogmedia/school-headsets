Noise-Canceling Microphones

Noise-canceling microphones offer a feature that is important for headsets in noisy areas such as classrooms and offices. These microphones are made for blocking out background noise which allows the user to concentrate on their work or listen music without distraction. They also aid in removing background noise and echo when making calls. They are available in a variety of sizes that are suitable for most head shapes and work with phones that have 3.5mm connections.

One popular technology for noise-canceling headphones is a dual-microphone design which employs two microphones that are directional to block out noises from around. The primary microphone is located close to the source of sound such as the user's voice, or music. The second microphone is placed to capture ambient sounds like air traffic and other sounds. When the two signals are subtracted from one other, much of the background noise is canceled out, leaving only the desired audio signal.

Another kind of headset with noise cancellation technology is hybrid technology. This headset utilizes a digital signal processor to examine the soundscape and decide what frequencies should be eliminated from the sound. The headset will then filter out the specific frequency. This is a more intricate technology than simple frequency response testing, and requires additional hardware, like a dedicated ANC processor.

Whatever the technology headset makers must ensure that their products deliver clear audio and clear speech. To achieve this, they should test the ANC functionality of headsets and their performance in real-world scenarios of external noise. They must also assess how well the talk-through feature functions. The HQS ANC Headset Test Suite from HEAD acoustics is the perfect solution for this.

This test suite allows manufacturers to accurately determine the ANC performance and active noise cancellation of headsets, headphones, and other devices. This test suite also includes several other tests that permit an exhaustive evaluation of each headset as well as its intended usage. These tests include distortion at high frequencies Acoustic insulation, passive and active insert loss and the effectiveness of a headset's "talk-through" function.
